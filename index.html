Process
Overview

Paracelsus is a project aimed at building a large-scale electrolysis facility with export potential in the northern Antofagasta Region, Chile. The project considers a three-phase development plan. Phase I is a 10 MW electrolysis facility, a dedicated off-grid PV Plant to supply a hydrogen powered freight train. Phase II consists of a larger 250 MW facility aimed at scaling Phase I to a wider range of local applications and Phase III consist of a 2.0 GW electrolysis facility and maritime port for export of H2 and derivatives.


Mining companies in Northern Regions of Chile are urged to achieved carbon neutrality in the years to come. The Project’s offtaker in Phase I will be a Chilean mining transport company whose locomotives are equipped with diesel combustion engines. The Project considers the adaptation of these locomotives to a hydrail (locomotives powered by H2 fuel cells).


The Project is pursuing engineering as well as other aspects of development such as the EIA, commercial agreements, permits and studies in order to achieve the RTB and proceed with the construction phase. Details on the process and timeline are described in the process letter that is attached to this Information Memorandum.
