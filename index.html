About Us

We started H2V because we see the importance of the production and export of green hydrogen that it means for the development of the energy industry in Chile. We bring together a group of different professionals with varied experiences areas that are the most relevant in the development of a project as large-scale export. Along the way we explore the different opportunities and restrictions that exist to produce hydrogen by weighing its advantages and disadvantages to come up with an optimal solution. We are located in the north where short-term local demand is compatible with future international demand for hydrogen. Our mission is to create a Hydrogen HUB located in Atacama where all production projects coexist and can be connected through a pipeline to a port for export and leave Chile within the main players worldwide in the production and export of H2.

Overview

Paracelsus is a project aimed at building a large-scale electrolysis facility with export potential in the northern Antofagasta Region, Chile. The project considers a three-phase development plan. Phase I is a 10 MW electrolysis facility, a dedicated off-grid PV Plant to supply a hydrogen powered freight train. Phase II consists of a larger 250 MW facility aimed at scaling Phase I to a wider range of local applications and Phase III consist of a 2.0 GW electrolysis facility and maritime port for export of H2 and derivatives. Mining companies in Northern Regions of Chile are urged to achieved carbon neutrality in the years to come. The Project’s offtaker in Phase I will be a Chilean mining transport company whose locomotives are equipped with diesel combustion engines. The Project considers the adaptation of these locomotives to a hydrail (locomotives powered by H2 fuel cells).

Project Overview
And Rationale

Global energy supplied by hydrogen is rising and there is an unlimited potential market to avoid Green House Gas emissions. Our proposal is to focus on strategic location of the region and current players and foster the cooperation and knowledge of multiple developers and existing companies of the Region, building a sustainable chain of production, transportation, and local demand of Green Hydrogen, aiming to exports to the world, developing the country and its people.
